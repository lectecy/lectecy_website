Ethiopian Guji

Geisha Village coffee is processed using the sun-dried method. First, the floaters and impurities are removed, then it is dried on African-style raised beds covered with thin layers of plastic cloth. During the drying process on a sun-drying bed, it will be screened again to select out coffee beans with insect damage or a greenish color. The total drying time is 18-30 days. Front Street believes that sun-dried coffee has a noticeable sweetness, gentle acidity, and a fruit-compound flavor profile.

Panama Geisha

The climate has made Panama's coffee. Panama borders Costa Rica and Colombia, and the east-to-west environment allows cold air currents to flow over the Central Mountains and converge at elevations of 6,500 meters or higher, creating a very unique microclimate in the Boquete and Piedra de Candela regions. This microclimate has ideal temperatures and rainfall for plant growth, and this area is the main producer of Panama coffee. In addition to the climate, the land around this area is rich in nutrients, and the fertile soil provides the perfect growing conditions for coffee, shaping a large number of unique, high-quality coffees.

Jamaican Blue Mountain

There are strict requirements for coffee plantations in Jamaica, and they are divided into Jamaican Blue Mountain Coffee (1000-1700 meters above sea level), Jamaican High Mountain Coffee (500-1000 meters above sea level), and Jamaican Premium Coffee (outside the Blue Mountain coffee growing area, below 500 meters above sea level). This means that only coffee beans grown at an altitude of over 1000 meters and planted within the Blue Mountain coffee growing area can be considered genuine Blue Mountain coffee beans.

Yunnan Coffee

991 years ago, the main cultivated coffee varieties in Yunnan were Typica and Bourbon, two classic high-quality coffee varieties. Later, Catimor series varieties were introduced from Kenya (with stronger resistance to viruses and higher yields). They are varieties of Arabica (also known as small-grain varieties). Because the two varieties have similar morphology and habits, they are often grown together. The most widely grown coffee variety in Yunnan Province at present is Catimor, while there are also a few Typica and Bourbon varieties in Baoshan area. Although they all belong to the Arabica species from a broad perspective, there are still significant differences between these varieties in essence.

Mandheling

Mandheling is an excellent coffee bean grown at an altitude of around 1,500 meters on highland slopes. The first-class Mandheling with the highest quality is produced in Takengon and Sidikalang. It has a pleasant acidity. Traditional bitterness, but not bitter, with a rich aroma of dark chocolate. The aftertaste is rich and very enjoyable, suitable for medium-depth roasting, and it exudes a strong aroma.
